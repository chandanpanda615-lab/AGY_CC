## Q4(a) — Financial assistance to buy own shares (5)

s.2(71), s.2(51), s.67(2), s.67(3).

| # | Step | Marks |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | s.67(2) — a public company shall not give financial assistance, directly or indirectly, by loan/guarantee/security, for purchase or subscription of its own or its holding company's shares | 1 |
| 2 | s.67(3) — exception: loan to **employees other than directors or KMP**, not exceeding **6 months' salary**, to buy fully paid shares held in beneficial ownership | 1 |
| 3 | (i) SSPL is a WOS of SBL → **deemed public** u/s 2(71). Sohan (Dy Marketing Mgr, ₹1L pm) → limit ₹6,00,000, so ₹15,00,000 **not valid**. Subarna is **KMP** (CFO) → **not valid** | 1 |
| 4 | (ii) At 25%, SSPL is not a subsidiary and not a deemed public company; but SBL (a body corporate) holds shares, so SSPL is outside the exempted class → **s.67(3) still applies** | 1 |
| 5 | (ii) Therefore the answer **remains the same** | 1 |
