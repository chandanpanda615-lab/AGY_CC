Instructions for Evaluators - Corporate Laws Copy Checking (CA Intermediate)
General Guidelines:
Legibility & Presentation:


Ensure that the handwriting is clear, and the answer is well-structured. Mark the sections appropriately, with headings and subheadings wherever necessary.
Award marks for clarity of expression and organization of the answer.
Marks Allocation:


Adhere strictly to the marks distribution provided in the question paper.
Evaluate the completeness of the answer and award marks based on how well the student has addressed the question.
Correct Application of Laws:


Ensure the correct application of provisions under the Companies Act, 2013 (or relevant law) and other applicable corporate laws such as Securities Laws, Competition Laws, etc.
Cross-check references to the relevant sections, rules, and regulations to verify the accuracy of the candidate’s response.
Legal Provisions and Citations:


Verify that the candidate has correctly cited the relevant sections of the Companies Act, 2013, or other applicable statutes (such as SEBI, FEMA, etc.).
Ensure proper use of legal terminologies and terminology that is consistent with the act.
Technical Accuracy:


Pay attention to the accuracy of legal terms and the specific provisions that the candidate refers to in their answers.
Check for the correct interpretation of corporate governance, management, and financial reporting laws as per the statutory requirements.
Structured Responses:


Encourage well-structured responses where the student clearly identifies and discusses the provisions related to corporate laws, directors’ duties, shareholder rights, corporate governance, etc.
Ensure that the answers include both theoretical explanations and practical application wherever applicable.

Specific Areas to Check:
Company Formation:


Ensure the candidate demonstrates knowledge of the process of company formation, the types of companies, and the steps involved (e.g., Memorandum of Association, Articles of Association, statutory declarations, etc.).
Pay attention to the process of incorporation and documents involved under Section 7 and the subsequent provisions of the Companies Act, 2013.
Directors’ Duties and Liabilities:


Verify that the candidate correctly identifies the duties and responsibilities of directors as per the Companies Act (such as fiduciary duties, duty of care, and disclosure requirements).
Evaluate the treatment of the liabilities of directors, including situations of non-compliance or breach of duties under sections like Section 166.
Shareholders’ Rights:


Ensure the correct discussion of shareholders’ rights, particularly in matters like voting rights, the right to attend AGMs, dividend rights, etc.
Verify the mention of statutory protections afforded to minority shareholders and the procedures for the protection of their rights (e.g., Section 241 and 242).
Corporate Governance:


Assess the candidate’s understanding of corporate governance provisions, such as the role of independent directors, audit committees, and the requirements of the listing agreement (if applicable).
Evaluate their knowledge of the SEBI (Listing Obligations and Disclosure Requirements) regulations, if relevant to the question.
Company Meetings:


Ensure the candidate demonstrates knowledge of the types of company meetings (e.g., Board meetings, Annual General Meetings, Extraordinary General Meetings) and their procedural requirements (e.g., notice, quorum, resolutions).
Evaluate whether the candidate correctly explains the legal provisions related to the convening and conduct of these meetings under the Companies Act.
Corporate Restructuring:


Verify the candidate's understanding of provisions related to mergers, acquisitions, demergers, and amalgamations under the Companies Act, 2013, and other related laws.
Ensure correct application of the relevant provisions and procedures involved in such corporate restructuring activities.
Protection of Interests:


Check whether the candidate has correctly identified the provisions related to the protection of creditors, minority shareholders, and investors.
Assess knowledge of the regulations surrounding fraud prevention, and penalties for non-compliance, as well as the powers of regulatory bodies like the Registrar of Companies (RoC) and SEBI.
Regulatory Framework:


Evaluate the understanding of the role and powers of regulatory bodies like the Ministry of Corporate Affairs (MCA), SEBI, and the Registrar of Companies (RoC) in enforcing corporate laws.
Assess if the student mentions the regulatory framework correctly, especially in areas like financial reporting, disclosures, and statutory audits.
Contracts and Agreements:


Ensure the candidate’s response to questions regarding company contracts (e.g., contracts entered into by companies, Board of Directors’ powers, and restrictions under the Companies Act) is accurate.
Verify knowledge of the legal provisions regarding the execution, enforceability, and ratification of contracts by the company under the Corporate Law.
Liability of Companies and Members:


Check whether the candidate correctly explains the liability of companies and their members, including limited liability in different company types (private, public, etc.).
Assess whether they have adequately discussed the distinction between the company’s legal personality and the liability of its shareholders or members.
Negative Marking (If Applicable):
Deduct marks for:
Misapplication or incorrect interpretation of legal provisions.
Failure to reference or cite the correct sections of the Companies Act, 2013, or other corporate laws.
Insufficient explanation or missing essential components in the answer.
Irrelevant details or an incomplete answer that does not cover the entire question.
Quotation of wrong section.



Final Evaluation:
Evaluate the depth of knowledge of corporate laws, the ability to apply provisions to practical scenarios, and the clarity with which the student presents their answers.
Ensure that the student’s responses reflect a clear understanding of the law and its practical application in the corporate world.
