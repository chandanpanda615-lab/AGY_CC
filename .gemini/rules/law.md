# Law — subject rules (CA Inter Paper 2: Corporate and Other Laws)

How to award for this subject. Shared mechanics live in `GEMINI.md`; the question paper
and the model answer outrank everything here. `.gemini/corporate_law.txt` is the subject
SOP — how to judge only.

## Syllabus

Paper 2 = Companies Act 2013 (Series 03 examined **Chapter XXII**, foreign companies —
so the scope is wider than ss.1–148), LLP Act 2008, General Clauses Act 1897,
Interpretation of Statutes, FEMA 1999. **The question paper decides scope, not this list.**
`.gemini/corporate_law.txt` also names s.166, ss.241–242, mergers/restructuring, SEBI LODR,
Competition Act — **CA Final**. Never require them; never penalise their absence.

## MCQs

- Key is in `scheme.md`. Correct option → full marks. Wrong → 0. Nothing in between.
- Marks per MCQ come from the question paper and **vary** — do not assume they are equal.
- No step marking, no reasoning credit, no warnings. A wrong option is simply wrong.
- **Struck-out work is not evaluated — the final answer stands.** Mark the option left
  standing; an illegible cancellation does not matter.
- Genuinely ambiguous final answer (two uncancelled options, unreadable letter) → flag
  `REVIEW`. Never pick the likelier one.

## Descriptive answers

- Step-wise, per `scheme.md`. Award against those steps, not an impression of the answer.
- Caps like *"1 Mark for each point max 5 marks"* are ceilings — honour them.
- Where the model answer gives an **"Alternate Answer"**, either version earns full
  marks. Never prefer the first.
- Award only what is on the sheet. Never complete the student's reasoning for them.
- Provision → Application → Conclusion, where the model answer uses it. Award each
  independently — a right provision with a wrong conclusion still earns the provision.
- The model answer's operative keywords carry their step.
- Wording need not match ICAI's. "Contention is invalid" and "contention is not tenable"
  are the same answer.
- Pointwise answers and ICAI terminology: reward, never require.
- Never exceed a question's allocation.

## Optional questions

Q1 is compulsory. Of the remaining questions, only the stated number count.

- Mark the **first ones attempted, in answer-sheet order.** A later one does not count
  even if it scores higher.
- **Sub-questions score independently.** Answering 2(b) but not 2(a) earns 2(b)'s marks;
  touching part of a question does not forfeit the rest.
- State in the evaluation which questions were counted and which were ignored.

## Never deduct — warn instead

Never subtract marks. The subject SOP's "Negative Marking" list is a list of **warning
triggers**:

- Wrong section quoted
- Misapplication or wrong interpretation
- Missing essential components
- Irrelevant padding

Each warning names the wrong practice, in that question. Marks earned are never reduced.

**Wrong section but sound reasoning → award the step, warn on the citation.**

A step never addressed simply isn't earned — that needs no warning.
