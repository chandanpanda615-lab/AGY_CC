# Advanced Accounting — subject rules (CA Intermediate)

Built from `.gemini/advance accounting SOP.md` (the subject SOP — how to judge only).
Shared mechanics live in `GEMINI.md`; the question paper and the model answer outrank
everything here.

## Syllabus / scope

The question paper decides scope. Where a question demands company accounts, the answer
follows **Schedule III of the Companies Act** (SOP §3 — final accounts).

## MCQs — carried from Law, confirm

The SOP says nothing about MCQs, so the evaluator's Law policy carries over:

- Key is in `scheme.md`. Correct option → full marks. Wrong → 0. Nothing in between.
- Marks per MCQ come from the question paper and **vary** — do not assume they are equal.
- **Struck-out work is not evaluated — the final answer stands.**
- Genuinely ambiguous final answer → flag `REVIEW`. Never pick the likelier one.

## Numerical / descriptive answers

- Step-wise, per `scheme.md` — award each component, never an overall impression
  (SOP §1, §2).
- **Formats carry their marks** (SOP §3):
  - Journal entries — correct account titles, correct debit/credit sides, narrations
    where required
  - Ledger accounts — balances c/f and b/f accurate; totals and balances match
  - Trial balance — debit total equals credit total
  - Depreciation and provisions — calculations and adjustments
  - Final accounts — classification of assets/liabilities/income/expenses per
    Schedule III; closing stock adjustment; net profit; ratio interpretation
  - Cash flow — operating/investing/financing categorisation; opening and closing cash
- **Steps and working notes earn marks even when the final answer is wrong** (SOP §2).
- **Consequential figures:** a number wrong only because it flows from an earlier error
  still earns its step if the method applied to it is right — the error is warned once,
  where it was made.
- Partial understanding and intermediate steps → partial marks (SOP §5).
- Minor spelling or grammar never affects marks unless it changes the meaning (SOP §2).
- Award only what is on the sheet. Never complete the student's working.
- Never exceed a question's allocation.

## Optional questions — carried from Law, confirm

The SOP is silent; the question paper's own instructions decide what counts. Where a
choice exists: mark the **first ones attempted, in answer-sheet order**; sub-questions
score independently; state in the evaluation which were counted.

## Never deduct — warn instead

The SOP says "penalize only for conceptual or procedural errors" and "clearly indicate
the reasons for deductions" (SOP §2, §5). Applied per the evaluator's established
policy: a conceptually or procedurally wrong step is simply **not earned**, with a
warning naming the error in that question — marks already earned are never subtracted.
Warning triggers:

- Conceptual error — wrong principle or accounting treatment
- Procedural error — wrong format, missing narration where required, misclassification
- Totals that do not cross-check (trial balance, ledger balances)

Brief positive feedback even where the answer is wrong (SOP §5) — the feedback page's
"what you did well" is not optional for a weak copy.

## REVIEW triggers (SOP §8)

- A discrepancy or ambiguity these rules do not settle → flag `REVIEW` and ask the
  evaluator — that is the SOP's "consult the head examiner" step
- Suspected copying or malpractice → flag `REVIEW` and report it; never adjudicate alone

## At upload (SOP §1)

Students share names. The evaluation records the student's name exactly as written on
the sheet, so the evaluator can match copy to portal account before uploading.
