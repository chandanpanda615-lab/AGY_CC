## Q2(a) — Beneficial vs Exceptional Construction (5)

**Cap: 1 mark for each point, max 5 marks.** The question asks for **any four** differences.

Opening distinction also earns as a point: Beneficial Construction **expands** the scope of
beneficial legislation to favour the protected class; Exceptional Construction keeps
exceptions/deviations from the general law **from being extended** beyond what the statute
provides.

| Aspect | Beneficial | Exceptional |
|---|---|---|
| Meaning | liberal/purposive; interprets to benefit the class the statute was enacted for | applied where a provision is out of the ordinary, an exception to the general law |
| Purpose | promote the object of welfare/remedial legislation, read liberally for beneficiaries | restrict or read narrowly provisions creating exceptions to general principles |
| Nature | liberal and inclusive | strict and narrow |
| Application | social welfare, labour, tax relief, protective statutes | provisos, exceptions, penal provisions |
| Presumption | legislature intended a benefit to a certain group | the exception is limited to what is expressly stated |
| Example | labour laws (e.g. Minimum Wages Act) read to favour the employee | tax exemption read strictly; ambiguity favours taxation, not the exemption |

## Q3(a) — FEMA: donations and pre-incorporation remittances (5)

FEM (Current Account Transactions) Rules, 2000.

| # | Step | Marks |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | (1) Donations by **persons other than individuals** need prior RBI approval above **1% of forex earnings of the preceding three FYs, or USD 5,000,000, whichever is less** | 1 |
| 2 | (1) 1% of USD 12,000,000 = **USD 120,000** | 1 |
| 3 | (1) So Bhargav Ltd. may donate up to **USD 120,000** without approval; anything above needs prior RBI approval | 1 |
| 4 | (2) Remittances exceeding **5% of investment brought into India or USD 100,000, whichever is higher**, by way of reimbursement of **pre-incorporation expenses**, need prior RBI approval | 1 |
| 5 | (2) 6% exceeds the 5% threshold → **prior RBI approval is required** | 1 |

Model carries a caveat: the answer presumes the remittance is reimbursement of
pre-incorporation expenses, and depends on 6% of investment vis-à-vis USD 100,000. A
student who reasons on that presumption is not wrong.
